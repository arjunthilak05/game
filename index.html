What is a Robot?

At its core, a robot is a physical entity designed to sense, process, and act upon the world. We can broadly categorize them by their operational intelligence.

Robot vs. AI: A Symbiotic Distinction

While often conflated, "Robot" and "AI" are distinct concepts. A robot is the body; AI is the brain. One can exist without the other, but their synergy unlocks true potential.

ðŸ¤–Robotics: The Body

Focus: Physical embodiment and interaction.

Components: Sensors, actuators, processors, physical chassis.

Function: Performs tasks in the physical world.

Intelligence: Can range from non-existent (remote control) to fully AI-driven.

Focus: Algorithmic reasoning and learning.

Components: Neural networks, data sets, learning models.

Function: Processes data, recognizes patterns, and makes decisions.

Intelligence: Is purely computational; does not require a physical form.
